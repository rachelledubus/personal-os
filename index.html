Why apps alone often don't stick

Notification habituation (reminders become background noise), equal-weight lists (nothing signals what actually matters right now), and passive triggers (a notification asks you to interrupt what you're doing) are the three most common structural reasons calendar/reminder apps stop working — not a discipline problem.

What this site is built around

Tiered priority (Dashboard caps "today" at 3), one place for stray thoughts (Quick Capture), and checklists that reset themselves instead of relying on memory (Routine, Habits, Chores).

Environment ideas worth trying

External memory over willpower — visible systems (this dashboard, physical checklists) consistently outperform "just remembering." Reduce daily decisions that don't need to be decisions — same breakfast, same workout clothes routine, same grocery order. Sensory-friendly defaults — since texture already matters a lot to you for food, the same principle can extend to clothing, lighting, or noise.
